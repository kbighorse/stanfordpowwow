Important Info Volunteers Should Know:
-Check-IN: Check-in 10-15 min.
-BEFORE your shift. Check-in for volunteers is at the Information Booth in the Main Arena. You will see it: Behind the main Stage w/ the big red canopies.
-We will have a sign in list w/ all your info, assmt., etc. but please try to keep track of these things as well. If you are working 4 hrs, you get a T-shirt and we will give that to you at Check-IN.
-Check-OUT: Please stop by the INFO Booth after your shift to Check-OUT.
-(Program Sales volunteers be sure to drop off the money you have collected and any unsold programs.)
-Attire: Wear comfy clothes and shoes.
-For those working during the day: It will be hot and dusty-Wear sunscreen, sunglasses, clothes to keep cool and bring a water bottle if you are in an outlying area like Parking and Camper Reg.
-For those working during the Night/Late afternoon: Nights get pretty cold-wear warm clothes, a jacket, etc.
-Snacks/Drinks: There will be a snack table behind the Info/Sales booth (by main stage). Before/During/After your shift you can go and get some drinks and small snack foods. Just a little reward for all your hard work! =o)
-Parking: There is no special volunteer parking. You will have to park your car in the Main Parking Lot or the Overflow if it is full. Main is off of Campus Loop and Over Flow is off Arboretum. There is a $5 donation being asked at the gate-but as volunteers of course you don’t have to give. (Still, it’s nice.) FYI: A lot of folks are just choosing just to walk or bike as well.
Job Descriptions:
Main/Overflow Parking: Duties include gate monitoring, donation collection, direction of cars within the lot (lining them up, etc.). There will be external security also helping with the lots. FYI: For those in Overflow Parking please not that there will be a split between where cars park and where people camping can go. Signs will already be up helping direct as well.
Vendor Check-IN: Duties include aiding the Booth Committee members in checking vendors in and directing them to their booth space. After checking in at the INFO booth, you will be directed to the area where vendors are being checked in: Ask for Umi or Jihan.
Arena Maintenance: Duties include keeping the Pow Wow grounds clean by changing overflowing trash bags, transferring full trash bags to dumpster. Other tasks involving the maintenance of the grounds may also be required as needed.
Program Sales: Duties include going throughout the crowd and selling programs ($3 each). The programs are really nice and will include a raffle ticket that can be entered to win a Pendleton (worth $100-200 each!)
Sales Booth: Duties include selling programs, t-shirts, sweatshirts, etc.
Info Booth: Duties include answering questions regarding Pow Wow, Stanford, the grounds, etc. Also checking in volunteers. There will be informative materials for Alumni, on Admissions, Pow Wow, etc. available.
Camper Registration: Duties include registering campers and collecting a small (donation) fee from campers. After check-in, you will be directed to the lot where registration takes place. Ask for David Brown or a member of the Publicity Committee.
