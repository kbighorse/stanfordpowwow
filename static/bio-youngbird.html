YoungBird Singers

Host Southern Drum

YoungBird, hailing form Pawnee, Oklahoma, has traveled to many powwows during the six years they have been singing together.  The group is made up of family and friends that are members of the Pawnee, Cheyenne, Kiowa-Apache, Sac & Fox, Crow, Navajo, and Comanche Tribes.

YoungBird has received many honors including: the first Southern drum to win the United Tribes travel trophy in Bismarck, North Dakota; the first Southern drum to win the Overall World Championship at Schemitzun 2000.  YoungBird was also invited to the 44th Annual Grammy Awards ceremony in Los Angeles, California in February 2002 for being one of six nominees for "Best Native American Music Album."  Recently, YoungBird was inducted in to the Oklahoma Music Hall of Fame, sharing that honor with the likes of Reba McIntire, Vince Gill, Garth Brooks, and many others.

YoungBird would like to thank the Stanford American Indian Organization for inviting them to be Host Southern Drum at this celebration.  They wish everyone "Good Luck, and have a good time and a safe trip home.  Ah-ho."
