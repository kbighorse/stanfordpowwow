Application hints:
* Answer question #6 honestly. If you have not been to a Native American event, please mention it. It's okay. In the past, some applicants ignored it. We just want answers.
* You may still apply if you do not have a California Sellers' Permit, however, you must let us know you have applied for one. You may not be a vendor if you do not have this permit.

Things to Remember:
* The Committee's decisions are final.
* Booth spaces are assigned at random.
* Requests to be placed at particular lots will be denied.
* Having been a vendor at a previous Powwow does not guarantee acceptance.
* You must have a valid CA Seller's Permit (California Tax ID). It must not expire during the Powwow. In fact, we require a photocopy of your permit. See below for more information on applying for a permit.
* Food vendors must have a Santa Clara County Health Permit.
* If you are accepted, you will be sent a contract and a list of rules/regulations.  This must be signed and understood before you will be allowed to participate.
* All booths must follow Fire Safety Regulations.  Information will be sent with the contract.
* The Powwow is an outdoor event. Booths will be outdoors in a clearing in the Arboretum on Stanford Campus.
* Vendors must provide their own tables and chairs.
* If you are accepted, you will be sent a vendor contract and a document of terms and conditions that you must accept.
